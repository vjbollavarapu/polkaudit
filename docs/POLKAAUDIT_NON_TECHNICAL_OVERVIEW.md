# PolkAudit – Simple Explanation Guide (For Non‑Technical Audiences)

## Purpose of This Document

This document explains PolkAudit in clear, simple language for non‑technical audiences such as business owners, community members, investors, regulators, and partners.

It is designed to help founders, team members, and partners communicate the value of PolkAudit without technical jargon.

---

## 1. One‑Line Explanation (Elevator Pitch)

> PolkAudit is a system that automatically tracks how public funds and decisions are made in blockchain communities and shows them in a simple dashboard so anyone can understand what is happening.

---

## 2. Simple Analogy

PolkAudit can be explained using familiar examples:

* It is like an online accountant and meeting secretary for digital organizations.
* It is like Google Analytics for how money and decisions are handled.
* It is like a public record office where everything is visible and searchable.

Example explanation:

> Imagine an organization where anyone can check the meeting records and bank statements at any time. PolkAudit does the same thing for blockchain communities.

---

## 3. What PolkAudit Shows (In Simple Terms)

When someone uses PolkAudit, they can see:

### 3.1 Decisions

* What proposals were created
* Who created them
* Whether they were approved or rejected

Similar to: Meeting resolutions

---

### 3.2 Voting

* Who voted
* How many supported or opposed
* Participation levels

Similar to: Election results

---

### 3.3 Spending

* How much money was spent
* Who received it
* Why it was approved

Similar to: Expense reports

---

### 3.4 History and Records

* All past decisions and payments
* Permanent records
* Downloadable reports

Similar to: Digital archives

---

## 4. Why PolkAudit Is Needed

Many digital communities and projects face problems such as:

* People do not know where money goes
* Decisions are difficult to follow
* Leaders are not transparent
* Trust is reduced
* Auditing is complicated

As a result, members and investors often feel confused and uncertain.

PolkAudit solves these problems by making everything visible in one place.

## 6. 30‑Second Demo Explanation Script

When demonstrating PolkAudit, use this structure:

> This page shows all major decisions.
> This page shows who voted and how.
> This page shows how money was spent.
> These reports can be downloaded anytime.
> Nothing is hidden.

Pause after this explanation to allow questions.

## 9. Words and Phrases to Avoid

Avoid using the following when speaking to non‑technical audiences:

* Substrate
* JSON‑RPC
* Indexer
* Async processing
* Database normalization
* API endpoints

These terms reduce clarity and engagement.
